CÁCH TRẢ LỜI CHUẨN HOÁ

• Part 1: Trả lời trực tiếp = paraphrasing the question

• Part 2: Expand the questions

Mẫu lý thuyết Do you think doctors should be paid more? Why?
Cách 1: List ideas (2 ideas)
Outline / Tiêu chí cấu trúc:

Topic sentence = Trả lời trực tiếp = paraphrasing question
Supporting sentence 1: Explain + example + Hypothesis/ Discuss
Supporting sentence 2: Explain + example + Hypothesis/ Discuss
Keywords: high-pressure job / hold lives in hands / many years of intense training

Sample Answer:

"Personally, I definitely think medical professionals deserve higher salaries. Firstly, their job is extremely high-pressure, as they hold people's lives in their hands every day. Secondly, they have to spend many years undergoing intense training, so a high income is a fair reward."

Mẫu lý thuyết Do you think doctors should be paid more? Why? (Góc nhìn khác)
Cách 2: Depends on (gender / generation / hobby / preference) → xem 1 vấn đề ở nhiều khía cạnh
Tiêu chí phân loại: Experience & Specialization

Outline: depends on years of experience / senior doctors vs graduates / specialization / surgeons vs general practitioners

Sample Answer:

"I think this disparity depends on experience and specialization. Senior doctors with decades of experience naturally earn more than fresh graduates. Furthermore, specialized fields like brain surgery require higher expertise, so their pay is much higher than general practitioners."

Mẫu lý thuyết Ví dụ sâu rộng về môn Sinh học cho Trẻ em
Cách 3: 1 idea: Explain + example + Hypothesis/ Discuss → Nói kĩ
Tiêu chí áp dụng: Deep dive (1 idea)

Outline: satisfy natural curiosity / understand the world / explore plants and animals / keep them hooked

Sample Answer:

"Yes, I absolutely think so. This is mainly because biology satisfies children's natural curiosity about the world. For example, when kids learn how plants grow or how animals behave, they feel connected to nature, which keeps them hooked on the subject."

Mẫu lý thuyết Ví dụ về sự thay đổi của phương pháp học
Cách 4: Give different contexts (In the past / Now - Currently / In the future / Person)
Tiêu chí áp dụng: Contexts (Methods)

Outline: past: passive learning, memorizing / now: interactive learning, trial and error

Sample Answer:

"Learning methods have changed significantly. In the past, people learned passively by memorizing facts from textbooks. Currently, people prefer interactive learning and trial-and-error, which helps them understand things deeply through practical experience."

DANH SÁCH CÂU HỎI HOÀN CHỈNH:

  1. What environmental laws does your country already have?
  2. Will there be a law that is universally accepted?
  3. How does technology affect the law?
Câu 1 What environmental laws does your country already have?
Cách 1: List ideas (2 ideas)
Gợi ý trả lời theo cụm (Outline):

anti-littering laws / heavy fines / regulations on industrial waste / factories processing water

Câu trả lời hoàn chỉnh:

"My country has implemented several green regulations. Firstly, we have strict anti-littering laws that impose heavy fines on people throwing trash in public. Secondly, there are regulations on industrial waste, forcing factories to process water before releasing it."

Câu 2 Will there be a law that is universally accepted?
Cách 2: Depends on (National interests / Economy)
Gợi ý trả lời theo cụm (Outline):

depends on economic status / rich countries: support strict carbon tax / developing nations: prioritize economic growth

Câu trả lời hoàn chỉnh:

"I think it depends on a nation's economic development. Wealthy countries easily accept strict carbon tax laws to protect the planet. However, developing nations might reject them because they need to prioritize economic growth and industrialization."

Câu 3 How does technology affect the law?
Cách 4: Contexts (Past vs Now)
Gợi ý trả lời theo cụm (Outline):

past: manually catch rule-breakers, inefficient / now: AI, cameras, track violations instantly

Câu trả lời hoàn chỉnh:

"Technology has transformed law enforcement. In the past, police had to manually catch rule-breakers, which was inefficient. Currently, with AI and security cameras, authorities can track traffic or environmental violations instantly and send fines automatically."
